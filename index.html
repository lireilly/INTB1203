Market Entry Research:
Using MarketLine Advantage

This tutorial walks you through MarketLine Advantage — the database you'll use to research companies and countries for your Market Entry Project — and builds a personal set of research notes you can download.

Duration: approx. 40 minutes

Format: Guided walkthrough + hands-on practice

Audience: Undergraduates working on market entry projects

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

Navigate MarketLine

Find company profiles and financial information.

Research Countries

Locate country-specific economic data and market analysis reports.

Evaluate Opportunities

Identify key data points for evaluating market entry opportunities.

Cite Your Sources

Export and cite MarketLine sources appropriately for your project.

What you'll do: Learn what MarketLine Advantage is → walk through researching a company → practice on your own company → walk through researching a country → practice on your own target country → review tips and support → download your research notes and a MarketLine quick-reference handout.

This will appear on your downloadable research notes. Please enter your full name to begin.

Step 1 of 7

Introduction

3 minutes

Connect to the Project

For your market entry project, you need to make two critical decisions: which company will expand, and where should they expand? MarketLine Advantage is a database that will give you corporate intelligence and country analysis to make informed decisions about both.

What Is MarketLine Advantage?

  • A professional-grade business database used by consultants and analysts.
  • Contains company profiles, industry reports, and country intelligence.
  • Updated regularly with financial data, SWOT analyses, and market forecasts.

You'll use MarketLine for both halves of your project: company research (who is expanding) and country research (where they're expanding to). Enter what you're considering so far below — it's fine if this changes as you research.

Step 2 of 7

Part 1: Corporate Research

15 minutes — demo

Follow along with each step below in your own MarketLine session — open the link, then work through Steps 1–4 before moving on to your guided practice.

Open MarketLine Advantage

Sign in with your Northeastern credentials through the library portal link above.

Step 1 — Accessing the Database

Log in through the library portal link above. Take a moment to look over the main search interface before searching.

Step 2 — Company Search

Search for a well-known company (e.g., Starbucks, Nike, or IKEA) to see how the database works, then filter your results by "Companies".

Step 3 — Company Profile Deep Dive

Open a company profile and walk through these four key sections:

Overview

Business model, products/services, geographic presence.

Financial Performance

Revenue trends, profitability, growth trajectory.

Operations

Current markets, competitive position.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths that could support expansion, plus weaknesses to consider.

Step 4 — Exporting Data

Look for the download or print options on the profile, and locate the citation tool (or note the report details you'll need to cite it yourself).

Key Questions to Consider
  • What are this company's core strengths?
  • Where do they currently operate? Are there obvious gaps?
  • What does their financial health tell you about expansion capacity?

Step 4 of 7

Part 2: Country & Market Analysis

15 minutes — demo
Step 1 — Country Reports

Search for a country report in MarketLine (e.g., "Vietnam" or "Brazil") and navigate to that country's profile.

Step 2 — Essential Data for Market Entry

These are the sections most relevant to your project:

Economic Overview

GDP, growth rate, economic stability.

Political Environment

Regulatory climate, ease of doing business.

Demographics

Population size, age distribution, urbanization.

Consumer Market

Spending patterns, middle class growth.

Infrastructure

Transportation, digital connectivity.

Industry Analysis

Specific industry reports for your company's sector.

Step 3 — Cross-Referencing Industry Reports

Search for industry reports by country (e.g., "retail industry in Mexico"). These reports show sector-specific opportunities and challenges within that country.

Making the Connection

Ask yourself: Does this country's economic profile align with your company's products or services? Is the market growing? What are the barriers to entry?

Step 6 of 7

Wrap-Up & Next Steps

5 minutes

Quick Recap

  • You now know how to find company intelligence and country data in MarketLine Advantage.
  • Use company profiles to justify your company selection.
  • Use country reports to build your argument for why a specific country is the right target.

Tips for the Project

  • Combine company strengths with country opportunities to tell a compelling story.
  • Use MarketLine to evaluate 2–3 countries before choosing your final target.
  • Keep track of reports and data for your citations.
  • If MarketLine doesn't have everything, note what other databases or sources you might need — and come back to the library for help.
